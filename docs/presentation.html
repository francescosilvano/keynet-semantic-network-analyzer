Climate Keywords Co-occurrence Network Analysis

Bluesky Social Media Study

Group 4 - Complex Systems

2025-12-15

Introduction

This study offers a detailed view of how climate-related and energy-related concepts are discussed on Bluesky.

Research Approach:

  • Collected posts including 25 selected keywords (shared + group-specific)
  • Analyzed how terms co-occur in the same posts
  • Constructed weighted networks reflecting online conversation structure

Key Insight: Each keyword represents a node, while edges describe co-occurrence frequency in posts.

Period: January 1, 2023 - November 25, 2025
Locations: California, Quebec, Norway
Total Posts: 4,957

Key Findings

1. Highly Interconnected Discourse

  • 38.4% network density indicates extensive keyword co-occurrence
  • Average path length of 1.6 means concepts are tightly linked

2. Five Distinct Thematic Communities

  • Policy & Mitigation (largest)
  • Weather & Environmental Impacts
  • Climate Science Fundamentals
  • Energy Systems & Technology
  • Misinformation (isolated)

3. Central Concepts Bridge Multiple Domains

  • “Emissions” connects policy, science, and impacts
  • “Natural Disaster” and “Extreme weather events” are most central

Key Findings (cont.)

4. Group 4 Keywords Reveal New Structure

  • Adding specific keywords (Ecosystem, Flooding, etc.) increased modularity
  • Revealed more nuanced community structure
  • Group 4 keywords bridge multiple themes

5. “Fake News” Remains Isolated

  • Zero connections to other climate keywords
  • Suggests misinformation discourse occurs separately from substantive climate discussion
  • Potential indicator of echo chambers

6. Strong Local Clustering

  • Global clustering coefficient of 0.645
  • Keywords form tight conceptual clusters
  • Indicates coherent thematic groupings

Implications

For Climate Communication

  1. Multi-faceted Discourse: Climate discussion integrates science, policy, impacts, and solutions
  2. Bridge Concepts: “Emissions” and “Natural Disaster” could be strategic focal points for integrated messaging
  3. Isolated Misinformation: Suggests need for targeted interventions to connect fact-checking with substantive discourse

For Future Research

  1. Temporal Analysis: How do co-occurrence patterns change over time?
  2. Sentiment by Community: Do different thematic communities show different sentiment patterns?
  3. Geographic Variation: Compare California, Quebec, and Norway separately
  4. Network Evolution: Track how adding new keywords affects structure

Limitations

  1. Platform-Specific: Results reflect Bluesky user demographics and discourse norms
  2. Keyword Selection Bias: 25 keywords may not capture full climate discourse complexity
  3. Language: English-only analysis
  4. Time Period: January 2023 - November 2025 may miss earlier/later trends
  5. Co-occurrence ≠ Causation: Network structure shows association, not causal relationships
  6. Location Keywords: California, Quebec, Norway may introduce geographic bias

Technical Details

Tools & Methods:

  • Data Source: Bluesky API

  • Community Detection: Louvain Algorithm
  • Minimum Co-occurrence Threshold: 1
